MOHRA

Machined Open Source Humanoid Robot
Senior Project 2024

Developing a full robot assembly from scratch, in conjunction with K-Scale Labs.

Prioritizing:

  • Easy-access Machining Methods
  • Minimized Cost balanced with Capable Performance
  • Open-Source availability, build with public toolsFull Analysis & Justification of All Design Decisions

    • Impetus

    Spurred on byh a connection by Joshua Huang to the K-Scale team, I and Joshua developed a project plan to create an open-source implementation of an aluminum-machined humanoid robot.

    At a basic level, this grew from a need by K-Scale to have their initial 3-D printed humanoid designs (Stompy) evolved into a more robust and applications-focused model. Leveraging the new proliferation of accessible CNC machining services, the model was to made primarily out of aluminum, and we in-scoped additional joints and transmission systems to improve overall robot capability and performance.

    Our initial development was inspired by the orignal Stompy model, various publications by the MIT Cheetah team (Katz, McKenzie), general military and research design teams producing humanoids.
